<p><p>The porcine vaccine market comprises several types, including inactivated vaccines that use killed pathogens to stimulate immunity, and live attenuated vaccines, which utilize weakened forms of viruses or bacteria. Toxoid vaccines target toxins produced by pathogens, while recombinant vaccines are created using genetically engineered microbes to provoke an immune response. Conjugate vaccines link polysaccharides to proteins for enhanced efficacy, and DNA vaccines introduce genetic material to cells for direct immune response generation. Each type plays a crucial role in swine health management.</p></p>
