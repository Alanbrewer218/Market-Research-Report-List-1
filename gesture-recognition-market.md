<p><p>The gesture recognition market is categorized into 2D and 3D gesture recognition systems. 2D gesture recognition primarily relies on surface-level interactions, utilizing cameras and touchscreens to track movements in two-dimensional space, commonly found in smartphones and tablets. In contrast, 3D gesture recognition captures gestures in three-dimensional space, enabling more complex interactions through depth perception, often employing advanced technologies like depth sensors and machine learning. Both types enhance user experience across various applications, including gaming, healthcare, and smart environments.</p></p>
