<p><p>The retinoblastoma treatment market is categorized into two main types: congenital (hereditary) and sporadic (non-hereditary) retinoblastoma. Congenital retinoblastoma arises due to inherited genetic mutations and often affects both eyes, requiring genetic counseling and tailored therapies. In contrast, sporadic retinoblastoma typically occurs in individuals without family history, affecting one eye primarily. Treatment strategies encompass chemotherapy, radiation, surgical interventions, and targeted therapies, and they may vary based on the type and stage of the disease, impacting market dynamics and product development.</p></p>
