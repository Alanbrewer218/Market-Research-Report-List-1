<p><p>Cellulose Acetate Flake (CAF) is a versatile biodegradable material derived primarily from natural cellulose sources, with two main types: wood pulp-derived and cotton-derived. Wood pulp-derived CAF is produced from sustainable forestry resources, offering cost-effectiveness and broad availability. In contrast, cotton-derived CAF, made from high-quality cotton fibers, is generally more expensive but prized for its enhanced purity and performance in applications like films and fibers. Both types cater to diverse industries, including textiles, packaging, and automotive.</p></p>
