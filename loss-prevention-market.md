<p><p>Loss prevention encompasses various market types that contribute to retail losses. Shoplifting involves external theft by customers, while return fraud occurs when individuals exploit return policies to gain refunds on stolen or counterfeit goods. Employee theft represents internal losses where staff steal products or cash. Administrative errors involve accidental mismanagement of inventory, leading to discrepancies. Lastly, the "Other" category includes miscellaneous factors affecting loss, such as vendor fraud or damage, collectively impacting a retailer's bottom line.</p></p>
