<p><p>Ceramic-based membrane filtration systems are categorized into three main types: microfiltration, ultrafiltration, and nanofiltration. Microfiltration systems remove larger particles and microorganisms, typically operating in the range of 0.1 to 1 micrometer. Ultrafiltration systems filter smaller particles, including macromolecules and colloids, ranging from 1 nanometer to 0.1 micrometers. Nanofiltration systems target even smaller contaminants, including divalent ions and small organic molecules, with pore sizes between 1 nanometer and 10 nanometers. Each type serves specific industrial applications based on desired filtration levels.</p></p>
