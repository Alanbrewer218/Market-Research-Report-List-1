<p><p>Water-based pigment dispersions are categorized into two main types: organic and inorganic. Organic pigment dispersions are derived from carbon-based compounds, known for their vibrant colors and transparency, making them ideal for applications like art and fine coatings. In contrast, inorganic pigment dispersions are composed of mineral-based compounds, offering durability and opacity, often used in industrial applications, coatings, and plastics. Both types are valued for their environmental benefits, as they minimize the use of harmful solvents in formulations.</p></p>
