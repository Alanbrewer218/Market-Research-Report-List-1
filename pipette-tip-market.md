<p><p>The pipette tip market is categorized into filtered and non-filtered types. Filtered pipette tips feature a built-in barrier that prevents aerosol contamination, making them ideal for sensitive applications involving volatile or hazardous samples. In contrast, non-filtered pipette tips lack this barrier and are typically used for general purposes where contamination risk is lower. Both types serve essential roles in laboratories, with the choice depending on the specific requirements of the experiment, sample type, and desired level of contamination control.</p></p>
