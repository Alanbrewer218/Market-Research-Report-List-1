<p><p>The medical surgical lasers market includes various types, each serving distinct purposes. CO2 lasers are predominantly used for cutting and ablating soft tissues, making them valuable in dermatological and surgical procedures. Argon lasers offer precision in ophthalmology and dermatology due to their ability to coagulate blood vessels. Nd:YAG lasers are versatile, used for deeper tissue penetration in conditions like tumors. Diode lasers are favored for their portability and efficiency, commonly found in hair removal and dental applications. Other lasers include pulsed dye and excimer lasers, utilized for specialized medical applications.</p></p>
