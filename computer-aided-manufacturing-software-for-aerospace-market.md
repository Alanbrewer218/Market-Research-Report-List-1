<p><p>Computer-Aided Manufacturing (CAM) software for the aerospace market is categorized into 2D/2.5D and 3D types. 2D/2.5D CAM focuses on flat and moderately complex parts, enabling efficient machining through layered approaches. It simplifies programming for components like panels and brackets. In contrast, 3D CAM supports intricate designs and geometries, essential for advanced aerospace components like turbine blades and fuselage structures. This type enables detailed modeling, simulation, and toolpath generation, enhancing precision and productivity in aerospace manufacturing processes.</p></p>
