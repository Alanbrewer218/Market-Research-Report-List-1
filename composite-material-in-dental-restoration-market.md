<p><p>Composite materials in dental restorations encompass various formulations designed for aesthetic and functional purposes. Resin-based composites are widely used for their superior aesthetics, bonding properties, and versatility, making them suitable for anterior and posterior restorations. Poly-acid modified composites, also known as glass ionomer hybrids, offer enhanced adhesion to tooth structure and fluoride release, which aids in caries prevention. Both types serve distinct clinical needs, combining strength, durability, and aesthetic appeal in restorative dentistry.</p></p>
