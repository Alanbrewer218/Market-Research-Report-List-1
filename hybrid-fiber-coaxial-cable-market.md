<p><p>The Hybrid Fiber-Coaxial (HFC) cable market primarily utilizes single-mode and multimode fibers. Single-mode fiber features a narrow core that allows light to travel straight down the fiber, enabling long-distance transmission with minimal signal loss. Conversely, multimode fiber has a larger core that supports multiple light paths, making it suitable for shorter distances and offering higher bandwidth and capacity. Both types play crucial roles in enhancing broadband connectivity and supporting high-speed data transmission in various applications.</p></p>
